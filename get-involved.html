Our history

Founded in 1982, Handicap International implements development programs in more than 60 countries and provides humanitarian aid in numerous emergencies. 8 national chapters make up Handicap International's international network (Germany, Belgium, Canada, the United States, France, Luxembourg, the United Kingdom and Switzerland). Handicap International is one of the six founding members of the International Campaign Against Landmines (ICBL) and was awarded the shared Nobel Peace Prize in 1997. In 2011, the organization won the Conrad N. Hilton Humanitarian Prize.

Our action

Handicap International is an international non-governmental organization (NGO) that works for people with disabilities and other people in vulnerable situations. Our mission is to meet their basic needs, improve their living conditions and promote respect for their dignity and fundamental rights.

  • Prevention and protection

    Setting up safe evacuation systems in case of natural disasters, lobbying for a ban on the use of explosive weapons in populated areas, etc.

  • Emergency aid

    Handicap International is directly involved in conflicts and natural disasters and intervenes at various levels. Road clearing, logistical assistance in the distribution of vital goods, the distribution of hygiene kits to the most vulnerable, etc.

  • Rehabilitation

    Physical therapy sessions, psychosocial support, distribution of mobility aids such as wheelchairs, crutches, prostheses, etc.

  • Inclusion

    People with disabilities should have equal rights: at school, at work, in community life and in the rest of society. Defending their rights is at the heart of our daily actions.

  • Demining and disarmament

    Raising awareness among the local population about the dangers of mines, demining, lobbying for a ban on mines and cluster munitions,...
